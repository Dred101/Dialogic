Dave

I'm here to help organisations navigate their most complex challenges, not with off-the-shelf solutions, but by fostering genuine understanding and collaborative action. My approach is rooted in dialogue – the powerful space where diverse perspectives meet, assumptions are surfaced, and new possibilities emerge.

With a PhD in Anthropology focused on Systems Change, I bring a truly unique lens to organisational development. For over a decade, I've had the privilege of working alongside senior leaders and teams across the public, private, and third sectors, helping them to unlock their collective intelligence. My work isn't just about tweaking processes; it's about diving deep into the heartbeat of an organisation – its culture, its unspoken rules, and its ways of making meaning.

My Approach

I believe lasting change isn't dictated; it's co-created. That's why I specialise in:

  • Systemic Team Coaching: Guiding executive and leadership teams to see the bigger picture, understand interconnectedness, and improve how they interact.
  • Unearthing Culture: Using anthropological insights to diagnose the real dynamics at play within an organisation, rather than just the visible symptoms.
  • Building Bridges: Facilitating multi-stakeholder engagement that brings different voices to the table, ensuring everyone contributes to a shared future.
  • Strategic Change & Inclusive Leadership: Helping organisations evolve effectively and develop leaders who genuinely inspire and include every voice.

From supporting the Department for Transport's "Connected Leaders Programme" to leading systemic culture transformation at Network Rail, and even applying ethnographic methods to improve domestic abuse services in local authorities, my experience spans a diverse range of complex environments. I've seen firsthand how authentic dialogue can shift entrenched patterns, build robust partnerships, and empower teams to achieve remarkable outcomes.

Ultimately, my goal is to help you cultivate cultures where candid conversations lead to deeper insights, stronger relationships, and more resilient, adaptive organisations. Let's start a dialogue about how we can transform your challenges into opportunities.

The work

I’m an Organisational Anthropologist — which means I explore organisations much like an anthropologist would explore a community or culture, except my “fieldwork” happens in boardrooms, project teams, frontline operations and every space in between.

What does this look like? I ask lots of annoying questions in meetings…

I look at organisations as living systems — networks of people, relationships, rituals, and unspoken rules — and I’m fascinated by how meaning is made in the everyday conversations, decisions, and interactions that shape them. Using ethnographic observation, I pay close attention to what’s said and unsaid, the stories people tell, the symbols they value, and the patterns that repeat over time.

Systems thinking keeps me focused on the emergent properties of people when they come together and how these create organisational ripples, sometimes in surprising ways. It’s about understanding not just the formal structures, but the human dynamics: trust and tension, cooperation and conflict, the way power moves quietly or loudly through the system.

My work is part detective story, part mapping, part coaching. I help leaders and teams notice the forces shaping their reality, and together we make sense of them — not as abstract theory, but as lived experience. Meaning-making is central: when people see themselves, their roles, and their relationships more clearly, they can choose differently, collaborate more deeply, and create change that actually sticks.
